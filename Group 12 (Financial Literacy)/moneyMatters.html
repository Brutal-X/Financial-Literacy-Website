SMMEs financing

Access to finance is one of the most significant barriers to small business growth in South Africa. Many small business owners struggle to access funding from traditional banking institutions due to a lack of collateral, credit history, or financial records. This makes it challenging for them to secure loans or other forms of funding to expand their businesses.

Findings indicate that sectors that are perceived as being too risky by banks, these are sectors with insufficient documentation which prevents banks from evaluating their creditworthiness, lack of or very limited knowledge of financial management, lack of understanding by SMMEs themselves as to why they are doing businesses they do, lack of awareness of the various products or services provided by different banks as well as a lack of proper security to cover their loans make banks reluctant to offer them (SMMEs) laons.

According to data on bank statistics provided by the South African Reserve Bank (SARB), the total amount of SME credit exposure to banks at the end of 2020 was ZAR 631 billion, or 25% of all company loans. Without taking into account other aspects essential to guaranteeing their success and sustainability, such as market access, business and management skills, and financial education, it is unlikely that the level of investment for SMEs will significantly improve.

Alternative Financing Options

Small business owners can explore alternative financing options like crowdfunding or venture capital to access funding. Additionally, the South African government has implemented numerous programs to support small business growth and development, including funding for small businesses, export promotion programs, and enterprise development initiatives.

What is crowdfunding?

Crowdfunding is a technique for obtaining small donations from a large number of people, generally using an internet platform, in order to raise money for a project, endeavor, or cause. It entails utilizing the aggregate financial assistance and donations of a network or community of people who are interested in or support the project or cause.

Crowdfunding websites act as online platforms that connect project creators or fundraisers with potential backers or supporters. These platforms facilitate the process of raising funds for a wide range of projects, campaigns, or causes. It's important to note that different crowdfunding platforms may have variations in their processes and features. Project creators and backers should familiarize themselves with the specific guidelines and terms of the crowdfunding platform they choose to use. To increase your chances of obtaining the necessary funds to bring your project to life follow the following steps.

  1. Research crowdfunding platforms: Explore popular crowdfunding platforms such as Kickstarter, Indiegogo, GoFundMe, or Patreon. Each platform has its own focus and guidelines, so choose one that aligns with your project or cause.
  2. Define your project or cause: Clearly articulate the purpose of your campaign. Whether it's a creative project, a charitable endeavor, a business venture, or a personal need, be specific about what you aim to achieve and how the funds will be used.
  3. Create a compelling campaign: Craft a compelling story and presentation for your campaign. Include relevant details about your project, your background, and your goals. Utilize images, videos, and other media to make your campaign visually appealing and engaging.
  4. Set a realistic funding goal: Determine the amount of money you need to accomplish your project's objectives. Ensure that your goal is realistic and justifiable based on the scope and requirements of your project.
  5. Offer enticing rewards: Consider offering rewards to encourage people to contribute to your campaign. These can be unique experiences, early access to your product or service, personalized items, or any other incentives that would appeal to your potential backers.
  6. Launch your campaign: Once you have prepared all the necessary information, launch your campaign on the chosen crowdfunding platform. Share the campaign link through your social networks, email contacts, and other relevant channels to generate initial traction.
  7. Engage with your audience: Actively engage with your backers and potential supporters. Respond to their questions, comments, and feedback promptly. Regularly update your campaign page with progress reports, milestones achieved, or any noteworthy news related to your project.
  8. Promote your campaign: Continuously promote your campaign through various online and offline channels. Leverage social media platforms, blogs, local communities, and press releases to increase exposure and reach a wider audience.
  9. Maintain transparency: Be transparent with your backers regarding the use of funds, timelines, and any challenges or changes that arise during the course of your project. Building trust and credibility is crucial for a successful crowdfunding campaign.
  10. Fulfill your promises: Once your campaign reaches its funding goal, ensure that you fulfill your commitments and deliver the promised rewards to your backers in a timely manner. Provide regular updates on the progress of your project even after the campaign ends.

Remember, crowdfunding success relies on effective storytelling, active promotion, and engagement with your audience. By following these steps and leveraging the reach of crowdfunding platforms, you can increase your chances of obtaining the necessary funds.

What is venture capital?

Venture capital (VC) is a sort of private equity and financing provided by investors to start-up enterprises and small businesses with the potential for long-term growth. The majority of venture capital is often provided by wealthy individuals, investment banks, and other financial organizations. The source of venture capital need not always be monetary. In actuality, it frequently manifests as managerial or technical expertise. Small businesses with outstanding growth potential or those that grow quickly and seem set to keep growing frequently receive VC funding.
