2. Budgeting and Investment Apps

Depending on your objectives, there are numerous solutions available. This can include managing your personal finances or keeping books for your small business. 3. Tax Preparation

With tax software programs like TurboTax or H&R Block, you can simplify the process of preparing and filing your taxes. These tools guide you through the necessary steps, identify potential deductions, and help you maximize your tax return.

4. Credit Monitoring

You may use technology to keep an eye on your credit score and get notifications when something changes or looks questionable. Credit reports, credit monitoring, and individualized advice for enhancing your credit health are all available for free through services like Credit Karma or Experian.

5. Online Banking

Most banks now offer online banking services, allowing you to access your accounts, view transactions, transfer funds, pay bills, and more, all from the convenience of your computer or mobile device.

Although technology can be a valuable tool for managing your finances, it's essential to remain vigilant about security and privacy. Always use secure networks and trusted applications, and regularly update your passwords and security settings.

HOW TECHNOLOGY CAN HELP SUSTAIN SMALL BUSINESSES ?

By increasing productivity, extending reach, boosting customer experience, and streamlining operations, technology plays a critical role in supporting small businesses. The following are some ways that technology can support small businesses:

Online Presence : Using websites, social media platforms, and online marketplaces, technology enables small businesses to create an online presence. By doing this, they may reach a larger audience, improve brand recognition, and provide accessible around-the-clock.

E-commerce: Setting up an e-commerce platform enables small enterprises to access customers outside of their immediate area by selling goods and services online. Online catalogs, secure payment gateways, and order management systems are just a few of the services that e-commerce platforms offer to help transactions go smoothly.

Digital Marketing: Technology provides a variety of digital marketing techniques, including content marketing, social media advertising, search engine optimization (SEO), and email marketing. These tools assist small businesses in connecting with and interacting with their target market, increasing traffic to their websites and revenue.

Cloud computing: Scalable, affordable computer resources are made available to small enterprises through cloud-based solutions. They don't require an expensive IT infrastructure or ongoing maintenance because they can save and retrieve data, work together on projects, and use software programs.

Online payment solutions: Thanks to technology, small businesses can now take credit/debit cards, mobile payments, and payments via digital wallets. Customers will enjoy greater convenience as a result of the decreased reliance on cash transactions.

Inventory Management Systems: Technology can be used by small organizations to make inventory management procedures more efficient. By automating processes like product reordering, sales report generation, and stock level monitoring, inventory management systems maximize inventory turnover and cut expenses.

THE EMERGING OF IoT

How is IoT relevant for business ?

The internet of things, or IoT, is a network of connected computing devices, mechanical and digital machines, objects, animals, or people that can exchange data over a network without requiring human interaction.

IoT devices easily remove process restrictions and solve conventional business challenges. As a result, firms are able to optimize everything, experience more production, cut costs, and do a lot more. Consider these 4 typical business needs that IoT provides excellent support for.

Access Control

rnet-enabled smart locks can be useful for manufacturing and retail businesses. These are the ideal instances of how IoT effortlessly resolves complex company issues. Businesses may regulate access to physical places without the usual limitations thanks to smart locks. Smart locks enable anything with just a few clicks, including changing passwords from a distance, tracking all accesses, and easily canceling access.

Monitoring/Event Recording

Smart cameras are an excellent example of how IoT devices can help small businesses. These Internet-connected video cameras may capture footage in high definition and upload it to the cloud. As a result, companies are spared the expense of purchasing closed-circuit cameras, which can cost between R1,000 and R4,000 each. Any company that needs to record extensive amounts of video cannot ignore the cost savings.

Dynamic Inventory Management

The usefulness of smart RFID tags for companies that deal in fast-moving consumer goods (FMCGs) is enormous. These little gadgets show how IoT can give businesses productivity advantages that have never been seen before.You can affix these reasonably priced RFID tags on goods. Then, portable devices can document activities like sales, returns, quarantine for quality assurance, etc. This significantly accelerates retail operations.

Equipment Maintenance

For many small firms, downtime is the scourge of their lives. Equipment failures typically take many hours to recover from, and they cost businesses on average R10,000 per hour.Imagine being able to do upkeep and repairs before any downtime occurs! That sounds magical, doesn't it? IoT sensors have that kind of power. IoT sensors can alert small businesses to an approaching breakdown so they can appoint a technician in advance to minimize downtime when they are configured appropriately. This is preferable to trying to control everything after a collapse has already happened.

THE UGLY REALITY

SMMEs run businesses in both the city and townships in South Africa. Concerns have been raised concerning their failure rate and incapacity to achieve a competitive edge, particularly in the current era of rapid adoption of information and communication technologies (ICTs).

Factors affecting ICT adoption in township SMMEs

According to the "The Southern African Journal of Entrepreneurship and Small Business Management" written by Dr Stella Bvuma and Carl Marnewick in terms of ICT adoption, South African township SMMEs appear to be falling behind and making no progress, as the majority of them are still limited to using just traditional business practices devoid of ICTs. Studies on ICT adoption haven't given township SMMEs much attention. Lack of ICT skills has been cited by various authors as one of the barriers towards ICT adoption (Jaganathan et al. 2018; Mwila & Ngoyi 2019; Yadav, Yadav, Malik 2019). The research has shown that the reason they aren't using alternative ICT is because they don't know which ones are best or most appropriate for their line of work and because they lack the skills required to get more familiar with potential ICT. Reasons for not adopting to ICTs include perceived high cost of ICTs, ICT infrastructure, and ICT awareness or knowledge.
